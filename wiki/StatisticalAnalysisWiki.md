<h1 data-section-id="uzczyb" data-start="254" data-end="305">Statistical Analysis of Mountaineering Fatalities</h1>
<p data-start="307" data-end="679">This section explores statistical patterns in mountaineering fatalities using historical expedition data. The analysis focuses on where deaths occur, who is most affected, when fatalities happen, and the most common causes. Together these insights highlight the risks associated with high-altitude climbing and how those risks vary across mountains, seasons, and time.</p>

<h1 data-section-id="dlt69t" data-start="2526" data-end="2548">Fatalities Over Time</h1>
<p data-start="2550" data-end="2634">Mountaineering deaths have increased over the decades, particularly after the 1970s.</p>
<p data-start="2636" data-end="2676"><img alt="Deaths Over Time" data-start="2636" data-end="2676" src="images/overtime.png"></p>
<p data-start="2678" data-end="2838">The rise in fatalities corresponds closely with the <strong data-start="2730" data-end="2803">commercialization and growing popularity of high-altitude expeditions</strong>, especially on peaks like Everest.</p>

<hr data-start="2840" data-end="2843">
<h1 data-section-id="639yr9" data-start="2845" data-end="2864">Seasonal Patterns</h1>
<p data-start="2866" data-end="2923">Mountaineering fatalities show a strong seasonal pattern.</p>
<p data-start="2925" data-end="2972"><img alt="Seasonal Pattern" data-start="2925" data-end="2972" src="images/seasonalpattern.png"></p>
<p data-start="2974" data-end="3131">Deaths peak in <strong data-start="2989" data-end="2996">May</strong>, which coincides with the main Himalayan climbing window when weather conditions are most favorable and expedition traffic is highest.</p>

<hr data-start="3133" data-end="3136">
<h1 data-section-id="1ykkaxt" data-start="3138" data-end="3155">Causes of Death</h1>
<p data-start="3157" data-end="3274">The most common causes of mountaineering fatalities are related to environmental hazards and extreme physical strain. Avalanches are hard to predict and altitude sickness can happen at any point on these mountains.</p>

<p data-start="3276" data-end="3319"><img alt="Top Causes of Death" data-start="3276" data-end="3319" src="images/causes15.png"></p>

#### Top Causes of Death

<p data-start="3345" data-end="3395"><img alt="Top Causes Pie Chart" data-start="3345" data-end="3395" src="images/deathcausespie.png"></p>
<p data-start="3397" data-end="3506"><strong data-start="3397" data-end="3421">Avalanches and falls</strong> are among the leading causes, followed by altitude-related illnesses and exhaustion.</p>
